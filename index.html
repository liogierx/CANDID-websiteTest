How does the CANDID study work?

As an observational study (and not interventional), participants are followed over several months during which they continue to receive their usual medications and specific care.

Participants with CDD and their parents have had a first visit at their clinic, called baseline visit. The follow-up study visits consist in either on-site visits or remote visits (done over the phone).

The study is planned for 3 years, as shown below.

More details on the study can be found on clinicaltrials.gov: NCT05373719

Where are our study sites located?

Overall, CANDID involves 22 clinical centers from 7 countries:



8 sites in USA 2 sites in Canada 1 site in France 2 sites in Germany 4 sites in Italy 4 sites in Spain 1 site in UAE

Study update as of January 2024

Enrollment was achieved in approximately 1 year, with 112 participants. Demographics, baseline characteristics, and baseline scale results were presented at the 2024 AES congress.
